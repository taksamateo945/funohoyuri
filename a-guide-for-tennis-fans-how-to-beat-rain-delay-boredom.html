A Guide for Tennis Fans: How To Beat Rain Delay Boredom

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Sunday, February 25, 2024

Whether it forces the mechanical roof to be brought into action or whether it stops outdoor play completely, rain is never a good thing in the world of tennis. This isn’t a sport that can take place during downfalls, which is why tennis fans, pundits, and players alike absolutely hate it when the heavens open!

Whether it forces the mechanical roof to be brought into action or whether it stops outdoor play completely, rain is never a good thing in the world of tennis. This isn’t a sport that can take place during downfalls, which is why tennis fans, pundits, and players alike absolutely hate it when the heavens open!

Tennis matches are stopped, and play is delayed when it starts to rain. There’s just no getting around this unfortunate truth. However, you do not necessarily have to sit there twiddling your thumbs while you wait for the players to step back on the court. There are a whole host of different things that you can do to beat rain delay boredom, three of which are detailed in the article below.

Play Bingo

There’s no better pastime than bingo when attempting to distract yourself from boredom. This highly immersive and incredibly engaging form of gaming is beloved by thousands of people across the globe, and for a good reason. When you dabble in a bit of bingo, you will instantly forget all about the rain delay; listening to the numbers that are called out, marking your score down, and shouting ‘BINGO!’ every time you get a line will become your primary concerns!

Don’t worry, you don’t have to trot on down to your local bingo hall in order to get your bingo fix. There are plenty of amazing 5 pound bingo sites to be found on the world wide web, three of the most popular being Viking, PlayOJO, and William Hill. You just have to find the online bingo platform that best meets your gaming specifications.

Dig Out An Old Tennis Video Game

Tennis video games may not be updated annually like soccer, basketball, and hockey games, but that doesn’t mean that this niche virtual sports genre can be dismissed. There are plenty of classic tennis video games still worthy of being played today… and they’re definitely good enough to be classed as boredom beaters!

When you’re in the midst of a rain delay lull, why not try digging out an old tennis video game?

Chances are, as a keen tennis fan, you will own at least a few of the classic titles listed below:

  • Hot Shots Tennis: Get a Grip
  • Mario Tennis: Power Tour
  • Virtua Tennis: World Tour
  • Grand Slam Tennis 2
  • Top Spin
  • Wii Sports

    • Listen To The Experts

    The tennis match may have paused for the time being, but that doesn’t mean that the broadcast will suddenly go off the air. As they wait for the roof to fit into place or for the rainfall to halt, the in-studio commentators will continue to provide their expert insights into the action. They will study how the players have performed, discuss the best shots and biggest talking points, and consider what they think may happen when the play recommences. If you’ve seriously got nothing better to do, you might as well pay attention to whatever it is they have to say!
