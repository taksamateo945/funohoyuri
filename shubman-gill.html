Career

Shubman Gill began playing cricket at a young age to follow in his father’s footsteps and accomplish his desire. He is a right-handed batsman who represents the Punjab cricket team and typically bats in the top-order. He is well-known for his ability to score big runs in a stylish manner. Similarly, his achievements at domestic events earned him a spot on the Under-19 World Cup squad as vice-captain.

He was permitted to participate in the Inter-District Under-16 competition in 2014, where he hit 351 runs and shared a record opening stand of 587. Following that, he was able to record an undefeated double century in the Vijay Merchant Trophy in the Under-16 state championship.

Additionally, to recognize these accomplishments, Shubman Gill was named BCCI’s best junior cricketer for two consecutive years. He also made his first-class debut for Punjab in the 2017 Ranji Trophy series. Gill then went on to hit a half-century on debut and an aggressive 129 runs in the following match. He also became a leading candidate for the national U-19 team and was named Man of the Series in the Youth One-Day International series against England.

Shubman was instrumental in India’s 2018 U-19 World Cup win. He finished the World Cup as India’s top scorer and earned the tournament’s player of the tournament title. Following this feat, Kolkata Knight Riders acquired him for INR 1.8 crores. And he was able to play in the Vijay Hazare Trophy, scoring 123 runs against Karnataka.

Gill was named India Blue’s captain for the 2019–20 Duleep Trophy in August 2019. And in October 2019, he was named captain of India C’s 2019–20 Deodhar Trophy team. Additionally, he became the youngest batsman for an Indian team to make a double century in a first-class match in the year 2019.

He also became the captain of India’s A squad for their trip to New Zealand in December of that year. Similarly, Shubman was named to India’s Test team in February 2020, this time for their series against New Zealand.
